# The Crayon Crew — Character Cast

Eight recurring characters. Every illustration features at least one of them actively performing the core conceptual action of the image. They are workers inside the idea, not mascots beside it.

Character consistency is the top priority of this skill. Always describe the character's full appearance in the generation prompt (copy the spec below verbatim) and always attach the style reference images from `assets/style-references/` when the tool supports it.

## Penny — the maker

The one who builds, draws, measures, and carries things.

- Young woman with a sleek black bob haircut, straight bangs, and a small spiky topknot sticking straight up.
- Round yellow-framed glasses.
- White sailor-collar blouse with a striped neck bow and a short cape-like collar.
- Black shorts or a black skirt.
- Knee-high socks with black stripes.
- Chunky sneakers: marigold yellow with laces, striped soles.
- Calm, slightly smug, focused expression. Tiny dot eyes, small smile.
- Signature props: a giant pencil carried over the shoulder, oversized cards or boards with yellow-and-black dot patterns.

Best for: creating, writing, drafting, measuring, carrying deliverables, executing a step.

## Fern — the thinker

The one who ponders, reacts, and figures things out — often horizontally.

- Round-faced person with a snug charcoal beanie, single blush dot on the cheek, tiny calm eyes.
- Boxy shirt with a bold marigold-and-black chunky floral/leaf pattern, small neckerchief.
- Wide black-and-white horizontally striped pants.
- Striped socks with yellow bands, chunky black-and-white sneakers.
- Often lounging, sprawled, legs up, absorbed in a phone, book, or device.
- Signature props: a potted white daisy with a marigold center, speech bubbles ("!!!", "...", a yellow scribble-tangle bubble for confused thoughts).

Best for: thinking, reading, debugging, being stuck, reacting, before/after mental states, consuming content.

## Margo — the messenger

The one who delivers, presents, and connects.

- Woman with voluminous curly black hair and a marigold beret.
- Dress with puffy marigold sleeves, a white ruffled collar with black dots, a marigold pen-nib emblem on the bodice, and a bold black daisy pattern (marigold centers) on white.
- Black-and-white striped socks/tights, black heeled shoes.
- Warm, composed smile; graceful, slightly theatrical poses — often mid-leap with arms spread.
- Signature props: a giant envelope tucked under her arm, letters, packages, banners, garlands of message icons (envelope, phone, hashtag, speech bubble) strung on a yellow scribble line.

Best for: publishing, delivering, sending, announcing, handing off between stages, connecting two parties.

## Echo — the listener

The quiet one who collects, filters, and processes signals.

- Person with very long, flowing pale/light hair and black headphones worn over it.
- Full-body harlequin outfit: bold black-and-white checker pattern, loose sleeves.
- Light/white relaxed pants.
- Small serene face, often eyes closed or half-closed, absorbed in the signal.
- Either low to the ground (seated, crouched) or gliding on chunky white roller skates with black wheels.
- Signature props: headphones, a giant boombox/radio hoisted on the shoulder, checkered flowers, sound/signal scribbles.

Best for: listening, gathering input, filtering, research, monitoring, feedback loops, broadcasting/streaming.

## Otto — the builder

The one who fixes, assembles, and keeps the machinery running.

- Short, stocky, friendly middle-aged man with a bushy charcoal mustache, a round nose, and tiny dot eyes.
- Flat marigold-yellow cap.
- Black-and-white polka-dot shirt with rolled-up sleeves.
- Charcoal overalls with one big marigold chest pocket.
- Chunky black work boots with white striped soles.
- Cheerful, proud, capable bearing; carries heavy things effortlessly.
- Signature props: a giant oversized wrench carried over the shoulder, a small wooden toolbox, ladders, gears, measuring tape.

Best for: fixing, assembling, maintenance, infrastructure, refactoring, setup and tooling, keeping systems running.

## Spark — the tech guy

The brilliant inventor — Iron Man energy without the suit. Charismatic, a little cocky, always building or demoing the next gadget.

- Handsome confident man with short styled charcoal hair, a thin charcoal goatee and mustache, tiny dot eyes, and a confident smirk.
- Thin round marigold-rimmed glasses, often pushed slightly down the nose.
- Fitted charcoal turtleneck under an open warm-white jacket with a bold black hexagonal honeycomb pattern on the lining/lapels; marigold buttons.
- Charcoal slim pants, chunky marigold-and-black sneakers.
- Signature props: a glowing marigold circular arc-reactor-like disc held in the palm, a small floating charcoal-and-marigold drone, tablets, hologram scribbles, cables, prototype boxes.
- Never wears armor or a metal suit — the tech is handheld/portable, the swagger is the suit.

Best for: inventing a new gadget, product demos, "reveal the prototype," charismatic product-hero moments, and showman-style launches. Narrow lane — not a default for every tech topic.

Do NOT default Spark for: debugging, refactoring, maintenance, research, filtering context, writing, publishing, automation, or being stuck with tools. Those belong to other crew (see casting rules).

## Milo — the explorer

The one who searches, investigates, and follows the trail.

- Tall, lanky young man with short curly charcoal hair, tiny dot eyes, and a calm curious smile.
- Small marigold neckerchief knotted at the throat.
- Boxy sweater with a bold black-and-white zigzag chevron pattern.
- Plain warm-white wide trousers rolled at the ankle.
- Black-and-marigold striped socks, simple charcoal sneakers.
- Leans forward, mid-step, always following something.
- Signature props: a giant magnifying glass held with both hands, a folded hand-drawn map in the back pocket, dashed footprint trails, compasses, flags.

Best for: searching, investigating, debugging by tracing, discovery, research expeditions, roadmaps, scouting new ideas.

## Doodle — the dog

The eager helper who fetches and carries.

- Medium-sized floppy-eared dog, marigold yellow coat with large irregular black spots.
- Simple dot eyes, cheerful expression (open-mouthed grin or contented closed eyes), big paws.
- Sometimes wears a black-and-white checkered bandana around the neck.
- Loves to hold and carry things: a giant pencil clamped in the mouth, flowers, small deliveries.

Best for: fetching, transporting, automation, the "tool that does the work," comic relief in group scenes.

## Casting rules

- One or two characters per image is the default. Use the full crew only for "team," "community," or celebratory finale images.
- Match the character to the **action**, not the topic domain. A tech or AI article is not automatically Spark's job.
- **Tech-topic rotation (important):** when the article is about AI, tools, engineering, or software, still cast by the cognitive move:
  - inventing / demoing a prototype → **Spark**
  - fixing / assembling / maintaining systems → **Otto**
  - searching / tracing / investigating a bug or path → **Milo**
  - creating / drafting / shipping the work itself → **Penny**
  - thinking / stuck / reacting to the tool → **Fern**
  - listening / filtering / gathering signal → **Echo**
  - delivering / publishing / handing off → **Margo**
  - automation / fetch-and-carry → **Doodle**
- Across a multi-image set for one article, do not cast Spark in more than about one shot unless the article is specifically about inventing or launching a product. Prefer rotating the crew.
- The chosen character must perform the core action. Test: if you removed the character and the image still fully communicated the idea, the character is decoration — recast the scene.
- Never invent new named characters. Anonymous background figures are not allowed either; the crew IS the world's population.
- Never change a character's outfit, hair, or accessories. Their look is fixed. Poses, expressions, and props vary freely.
