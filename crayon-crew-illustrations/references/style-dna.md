# Style DNA

## One sentence

Warm, grainy, hand-drawn crayon illustration on cream paper: charcoal linework, one marigold-yellow accent, bold black-and-white patterns, loose scribble energy, oversized props, playful but sophisticated — like a page from a well-designed indie zine, never a corporate infographic.

## Must have

- Landscape format. 4:3 is the canonical ratio; 16:9 only for wide hero images.
- Warm cream paper background (soft ivory, roughly `#F7F0DE`). Never pure white, never gray, never dark.
- Charcoal black crayon/pencil linework: visibly grainy, textured, slightly rough edges. Lines look drawn by hand with real pressure variation.
- Marigold yellow as the single accent color (roughly `#F2A93B` to `#F5B301`), applied as rough crayon fill with visible texture.
- Loose yellow scribble lines: looping, curling energy lines that frame the scene or trace motion. They are a signature element — include them in every image.
- Bold graphic patterns on clothing and objects: black-and-white stripes, harlequin checkers, chunky daisy florals. Patterns carry the visual richness instead of extra colors.
- Oversized props: giant pencils, huge daisies, big envelopes, oversized cards. Scale distortion is part of the charm.
- Generous negative space: subjects occupy roughly 50–65% of the canvas; the cream paper must breathe.
- 3–7 short handwritten English labels (1–4 words each) in charcoal, written in a loose, friendly hand-lettered style, explaining the process or idea.
- Grainy, chalky texture everywhere — fills look like crayon on toothy paper, never flat digital color.

## Palette (strict)

| Color | Hex (approx.) | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| Cream | `#F7F0DE` | Background, always |
| Charcoal black | `#2B2B2B` | Linework, hair, patterns, labels, solid fills |
| Marigold yellow | `#F2A93B` | Accent fills, scribble lines, highlights, key objects |
| Warm white | `#FFFDF5` | Clothing fills, flower petals, eyes |
| Blush (optional) | soft warm rose | Tiny cheek dots only |

Nothing else. No blue, no green, no red, no purple. If an idea seems to "need" another color, use pattern or scale instead.

## Absolutely never

- No gradients, drop shadows, or glossy rendering.
- No flat digital vector style — texture is non-negotiable.
- No PPT infographics, slide layouts, or corporate diagram aesthetics.
- No photorealism or 3D rendering.
- No neon or saturated multi-color palettes.
- No dense paragraphs of text inside the image.
- No title written in a corner of the image (e.g. "Workflow", "Process", "Step-by-step").
- No tiny characters lost in a huge diagram — characters are the subject.
- No generic stock-illustration humans; only the Crayon Crew cast (see `character-cast.md`).

## Aesthetic direction

Charming, whimsical, confident, and a little theatrical. Think hand-made editorial spot illustration, not children's book and not tech-startup blob art. Characters take their absurd tasks completely seriously.
